Manifesto // Fractured Panels

Build atmosphere first. Let interaction arrive like thunder after the rain.

01

Monochrome as tension

Most of the interface lives in black, bone, and fogged charcoal. Crimson appears only when the page wants to cut through silence.

02

Broken geometry

Panels lean, collide, and overlap so the page feels edited by blades, not boxes.

The story is the navigation.

Instead of a neutral landing page, the visitor moves through scenes, pressure, and pause. Sections should feel sequential, cinematic, and inevitable.

"Not every hero needs color. Some arrive in ink, static, and rain."

Panel Showcase // Pseudo 3D Depth

Characters cut out of the storm, suspended between poster art and memory.

Panel A

Split gaze

Sharp framing, diagonal borders, and an off-axis stare that anchors the left side of the story.

Panel B

Lone center

The central silhouette becomes the emotional pivot, framed by a false moon and drifting debris.

Panel C

Afterimage

Negative space and soft rim-light keep the darker panel readable while preserving the manga grit.

Ritual // Motion Language

The scroll should feel like panels slipping loose from a printed page.

GSAP staging

Pinned hero tension, staggered type, and scene wipes that do not overpower the composition.

Particle weather

Rain, ash, and ink specks drift above the interface with density tuned down on smaller screens.

Hover depth

Only the cutouts and showcase panels react, keeping interactivity sharp and selective.

Finale // Closing Frame

Leave the viewer in the quiet after the strike.

The final section slows the rhythm, softens the noise, and lands the concept with a single controlled accent.
